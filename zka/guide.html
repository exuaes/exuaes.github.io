Practice Prompt

Write an article for your school magazine about whether teenagers spend too much time on social media. Give your opinion and reasons. (~200–250 words)
Model Answer · ~220 words
Are We Addicted to Our Phones? Have you ever sat down for dinner only to realize everyone at the table is staring at their screen? Social media has become a huge part of teenage life – but is that really a problem? On one hand, platforms like Instagram and TikTok help young people stay connected with friends, discover new interests, and even learn things in creative ways. Many teenagers use these apps to share their art, music, or opinions with the world. That is genuinely valuable. However, there is a downside too. Studies show that teenagers who spend more than three hours a day on social media are more likely to feel anxious or lonely. Scrolling through perfectly edited photos can make people feel like their own lives are not good enough. Moreover, time spent online is often time not spent sleeping, exercising, or talking face-to-face. In my opinion, social media itself is not the enemy – it is how we use it. Setting limits, such as no phones after 10 pm or during meals, can make a real difference. Schools could also help by teaching digital literacy. So yes, many of us probably spend too much time online. But with a little self-awareness, we can enjoy the benefits without losing ourselves in the scroll. What do you think? Are you in control of your screen time?

Email / Letter

Formal or informal · Know your audience

Structure (Formal)

①
SalutationDear Mr/Ms [Name], / Dear Sir or Madam,
②
OpeningState your reason for writing clearly
③
Main BodyDetails, request, or explanation (2 paragraphs)
④
ClosingThank them + next step + "Yours sincerely,"
Informal: "Hi [Name]!" → casual tone → "Best wishes," or "Take care,"

Useful Phrases

  • Formal: I am writing to enquire about…
  • I would be grateful if you could…
  • I look forward to hearing from you.
  • Please do not hesitate to contact me.
  • Informal: How are you? It's been ages!
  • I just wanted to tell you that…
  • Let me know what you think!
  • Can't wait to hear from you.

Practice Prompt

Write a formal email to your school principal suggesting that the school should introduce a student garden project. Explain your idea and give reasons. (~200–250 words)
Model Answer · ~215 words
Dear Ms Schmidt, I am writing to suggest an idea that I believe could benefit our school community greatly: a student garden project on the school grounds. The idea would be to convert the unused area next to the sports hall into a small vegetable and herb garden. Students from different year groups could take turns caring for it during breaks or in a dedicated weekly slot. The produce could then be used in the school canteen or donated to local families in need. I think this project would have many advantages. First of all, it would give students a hands-on experience of where food comes from, which connects well to topics we discuss in biology and geography. It would also encourage teamwork and responsibility. Furthermore, spending time outdoors and doing something practical has been shown to reduce stress and improve concentration. Of course, there would be costs involved, such as tools, seeds, and raised beds. However, I believe we could apply for a small environmental grant or ask local businesses to sponsor the project. Many schools in Germany have done this successfully. I would be very happy to discuss this idea further and even help organise a small planning group of interested students. Thank you very much for taking the time to read this email. I look forward to hearing your thoughts. Yours sincerely, [Your Name]

Practice Prompt

Write a comment on the following statement: "School uniforms should be compulsory in all schools." Do you agree or disagree? Give reasons. (~200–250 words)
Model Answer · ~225 words
Should all students be forced to wear uniforms? In my opinion, making school uniforms compulsory would do more harm than good. First and foremost, clothing is one of the few ways teenagers can express their personality. At a time when young people are figuring out who they are, being able to choose what they wear helps build confidence and individuality. Forcing everyone into the same outfit takes that away. Another important point is that uniforms do not actually solve the problems they are meant to address. Bullying, for example, does not disappear just because everyone wears the same clothes – it simply finds other outlets, such as shoes, hairstyles, or accessories. The idea that uniforms create equality is therefore somewhat misleading. Admittedly, some people argue that uniforms reduce pressure on families who cannot afford the latest fashion trends. This is a valid concern. However, a simpler solution would be to have a relaxed dress code rather than a strict uniform, allowing flexibility while still keeping things affordable and appropriate. All things considered, I believe that schools should focus on creating a positive and respectful environment through good teaching and strong values – not through what students wear. Personal freedom matters, and it starts with small choices like getting dressed in the morning. Schools should trust their students to dress appropriately and focus on what really counts: learning.

Review / Report

Review = evaluate · Report = inform + recommend

Structure (Review)

①
IntroductionWhat are you reviewing? Brief context
②
PositivesWhat worked well and why
③
NegativesWhat could be improved
④
RecommendationWould you recommend it? To whom?
Report: Use headings (Introduction / Findings / Recommendation) + neutral, factual tone

Useful Phrases

  • Review: Overall, I would highly recommend…
  • One of the highlights was definitely…
  • On the downside, …
  • Despite its flaws, … is well worth…
  • I would give it ★★★★ out of five.
  • Report: The aim of this report is to…
  • The findings suggest that…
  • Based on the results, I recommend that…

Practice Prompt

Write a review of a film, book, or event you recently experienced. Describe it, evaluate it, and say whether you would recommend it. (~200–250 words)
Model Answer · ~230 words
Review: "The Martian" (Film) "The Martian" is a 2015 science fiction film directed by Ridley Scott, starring Matt Damon as astronaut Mark Watney, who is accidentally left behind on Mars and must survive alone until a rescue mission can reach him. One of the highlights of the film is undoubtedly its tone. Despite the terrifying premise, the story is surprisingly funny and uplifting. Mark Watney's optimism and problem-solving mindset make him a genuinely likeable character, and watching him "science the heck out of" every problem is both entertaining and inspiring. The visuals are stunning, with wide shots of the Martian landscape creating a real sense of isolation and scale. On the downside, some of the science has been simplified for dramatic effect, which may frustrate viewers with a strong interest in physics or chemistry. A few subplots involving NASA and the media also feel a little rushed and could have been developed more fully. Overall, however, "The Martian" is an engaging and feel-good film that manages to make space exploration genuinely exciting. It is suitable for teenagers and adults alike, and it might even spark an interest in science. I would give it four out of five stars and highly recommend it – especially to anyone who enjoys problem-solving stories or simply wants a film that leaves them feeling good about human ingenuity.

General Writing Tips

📝 Word Count Aim for 200–250 words. Under 180 is too short. Over 280 wastes time and loses focus.
🎯 Address the task Always re-read the prompt. Make sure you answer every part of the question before you finish.
🔗 Connectors Use linking words: however, furthermore, on the other hand, therefore, as a result, in addition, admittedly, nevertheless.
✅ Check tone Formal = no contractions (don't → do not). Informal = natural and friendly. Always match the audience.
📐 Paragraph structure Every paragraph = one main idea. Topic sentence → development → example or reason. Never mix two ideas in one paragraph.
⏱ Time management Spend ~5 min planning, ~20 min writing, ~5 min checking. Fixing grammar errors at the end is worth it.
💡 Vocabulary variety Avoid repeating the same word. Use synonyms: good → excellent / beneficial / valuable · bad → harmful / problematic / concerning.
🗂 Dictionary use A dictionary IS allowed. Use it to check spelling or find a better word – not to translate whole sentences.
